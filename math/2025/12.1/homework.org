Find two solutions (not multiple of each other) of the Bessel equation of order $\frac{3}{2}$.
$x^2y'' + xy' + \left(x^2 - \frac{9}{4}\right) y = 0,\quad x>0$
here $v=\frac{3}{2}$
so we can assume $y=\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_nx^{n+\frac{3}{2}}$
then $y' = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n(n+\frac{3}{2})x^{n+\frac{1}{2}}$
$y'' = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n(n+\frac{3}{2})(n+\frac{1}{2})x^{n-\frac{1}{2}}$
so $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n(n+\frac{3}{2})(n+\frac{1}{2})x^{n+\frac{3}{2}} + \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n(n+\frac{3}{2})x^{n+\frac{3}{2}} + \left(x^2-\frac{9}{4}\right)\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_nx^{n+\frac{3}{2}} = 0$
assume $x\neq 0$, then
$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n(n+\frac{3}{2})(n+\frac{1}{2}) + \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n(n+\frac{3}{2}) + \left(x^2-\frac{9}{4}\right)\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n = 0$
$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n(n+\frac{3}{2})(n+\frac{1}{2}) + a_n(n+\frac{3}{2}) + \left(x^2-\frac{9}{4}\right) a_n= 0$
$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n(n^2+3n+\frac{9}{4} + \left(x^2-\frac{9}{4}\right)) = 0$
$\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n(n^2 + 3n + x^2) = 0$
for this to work, transform to $\sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_n(n^2 + 3n) + \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} a_{n+1} = 0$
so we know $a_n(n^2+3n) + a_{n+1} = 0$
assume $a_1$ is known constant
then $a_2 = -4a_1$, $a_3 = 10\cdot 4a_1$
so for $n\geq 2$, $a_n = (-1)^{n-1} \left(\prod_{i=1}^{n-1} i(i+3)\right) a_1$
so $y=a_0x^{\frac{3}{2}} + a_1x^{\frac{5}{3}} + \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (-1)^{n-1} \left(\prod_{i=1}^{n-1} i(i+3)\right) a_1 x^{n+\frac{3}{2}}$
assign $a_0, a_1$ with $0, 1$ and $1, 0$
we get $y_1 = x^{\frac{3}{2}}, y_2 = x^{\frac{5}{3}} + \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} (-1)^{n-1} \left(\prod_{i=1}^{n-1} i(i+3)\right) x^{n+\frac{3}{2}}$
** 8
Consider the Bessel equation of order $v$
$$x^2y'' + xy' + (x^2 - v^2)y = 0,\quad x>0$$
where $v$ is real and positive
*** a
Show that $x=0$ is a regular singular point and that the roots of the indicial equation are $v$ and $-v$
From $x^2=0$ we know $x=0$ is singular
From $\frac{1}{x} \to 0, 1-\frac{v^2}{x^2} \to 1$ we know $x=0$ is regular
Let $y=x^r$
then $x^2r(r-1)x^{r-2} + xrx^{r-1} + (x^2-v^2) x^r = 0$
so $r^2 - r + r - v^2 + x^2 = 0$
as $x\to 0$, we know $r^2 - v^2 = 0$, giving roots $\pm v$
*** b
Corresponding to the larger root $v$. Show that one solution is
$$y_1(x) = x^v \left(1 - \frac{1}{1!(1+v)}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)^2 + \frac{1}{2!(1+v)(2+v)}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)^4 + \sum_{m=3}^{\infty}\frac{(-1)^m}{m!(1+v)\dots (m+v)}\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)^{2m}\right)$$
suppose $y(x) = x^v\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_nx^n$
$y' = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n (n+v)x^{n+v-1}$
$y'' = \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n (n+v)(n+v-1)x^{n+v-2}$
substitute back we have $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n (n+v)(n+v-1)x^{n+v} + \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n (n+v)x^{n+v} + (x^2-v^2) \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_nx^{n+v} = 0$
as we don't consider $x=0$,
there is $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n (n+v)(n+v-1) + \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n (n+v) + (x^2-v^2) \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n = 0$
and when $x\to v$, $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n (n+v-1) + \sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n = 0$
which further reduces to $\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n (n+v) = 0$
this means any ${a_n}$ suiting would work as a solution
in the example, $a_{odd}=0$
for $a_{even}$, $a_0=1, a_2=-\frac{1}{2^2\cdot 1!(1+v)}, a_4=\frac{1}{2^4\cdot 2!(1+v)(2+v)},\dots$

$$\begin{aligned}
\sum_{n=0}^{\infty} a_n (n+v)
