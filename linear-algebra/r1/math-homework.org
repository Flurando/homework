* Problem 4
$$\left[\begin{array}{cccc|c}
1 & 3 & -1 & 2 & 5\\
2 & 6 & -3 & 1 & 8\\
3 & 9 & -4 & 3 & 13
\end{array}\right]$$
$$\left[\begin{array}{cccc|c}
1 & 3 & -1 & 2 & 5\\
0 & 0 & -1 & -3 & -2\\
0 & 0 & -1 & -3 & -2
\end{array}\right]$$
$$\left[\begin{array}{cccc|c}
1 & 3 & -1 & 2 & 5\\
0 & 0 & -1 & -3 & -2\\
0 & 0 & 0 & 0 & 0
\end{array}\right]$$
leading variables are $x_1, x_3$, free variables are $x_2, x_4$
suppose $x_1 = a, x_3 = b$
then $x_4 = \frac{2-b}{3}$
we know $a + 3x_2 - b + \frac{4-2b}{3} = 5$
which means $x_2 = \frac{11 - 3a + 5b}{9}$
so general solution is $\begin{bmatrix}a\\\frac{11 - 3a + 5b}{9}\\b\\\frac{2-b}{3}\end{bmatrix}$
or written as $\begin{bmatrix}9a\\11 - 3a + 5b\\9b\\6-3b\end{bmatrix}$
